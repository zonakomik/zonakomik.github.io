1. write a story about "School" dan terjemahan bahasa indonesianya


PART = DESCRIPTIVE TEXT

------------------

Jawaban :

My name is Dee. I’m thirteen years old. I study at SMPN  “Brainly Nuansa Indah.” My school is elite and very famous in my town. Many students come from different regions study in this school. ( IDENTIFICATION )

My school is green and shady. There are many variety of plants and flowers. The trees produce the fresh air around the school and it makes more comfortable to stay. My school has many rooms . There are a spacious library , a large parking shade, a headmaster’s office, school canteens, language laboratories, science laboratories, multimedia rooms, toilets, a hall and twenty rooms for seventh grade class until ninth grade class. My school’s yard is very large. It is in front of the ninth grade classes. We can play kinds of sport games in the school’s yard. My school provides the sport equipment completely because my school creates the sport clubs to train for those who are talented in the sport game.  ( DESCRIPTION )

When we enter the school,   we will see the greeting billboard which is written “Welcome to SMPN “Brainly Nuansa Indah”  in front of the school building gate. There is a security post beside the gate. There are two securities guard the post. They have a responsibility to keep our school safe. That is a story about my school. I love my school very much because of its conduciveness. ( DESCRIPTION )

TRANSLATION

Nama ku Dee.  Aku berusia tiga belas tahun. Aku bersekolah  di SMPN "Brainly Nuansa Indah." Sekolah ku  elit dan sangat terkenal di kota ku. Banyak siswa datang dari berbagai daerah untuk belajar  di sekolah ini.  

Sekolahku  hijau dan teduh. Ada beragam  tanaman dan bunga. Pohon-pohon memberikan udara segar di sekitar sekolah dan itu membuat lebih nyaman untuk menetap. Sekolah ku  memiliki banyak ruangan. Ada perpustakaan yang luas, tempat parkir yang luas, kantor kepala sekolah, kantin sekolah, laboratorium bahasa, laboratorium sains, ruang multimedia, toilet, aula, dan dua puluh ruangan untuk kelas tujuh hingga kelas sembilan. Halaman sekolah ku sangat luas. Halaman letaknya di depan kelas sembilan. Kami dapat memainkan berbagai jenis permainan olahraga di halaman sekolah. Sekolah ku  menyediakan peralatan olahraga dengan lengkap karena sekolah ku membuat  klub-klub  olahraga untuk melatih bagi mereka yang berbakat dalam permainan olahraga.

Ketika kami  memasuki sekolah, kami akan melihat papan pengumuman ucapan yang tertulis "Selamat datang di SMPN" Brainly Nuansa Indah "di depan gerbang gedung sekolah. Ada pos keamanan di samping gerbang. Ada dua satpam  yang menjaga pos. Mereka memiliki tanggung jawab untuk menjaga keamanan sekolah kami . Itu adalah cerita tentang sekolah ku. Aku sangat mencintai sekolah ku karena kondusifitasnya ( damai )

3. Conclusion

11. Make a story about you??


Astraphobia (fear of thunder & lightning) — sad

My cheeks were streaked with tears as another lightning struck. I was waiting for the thunder to follow as I hugged myself into the corner of my bed. For most people, they might fear the sight of lightning and the sound of thunder but, I, I was reminded of a sorrowful memory. Imagine having to go through all the tears and tissues everytime it rains. Having to relive the painful memory of losing those you love. All at once, gone. They were gone.

It was the fall of October 1999, the kitchen was warm and homely with the inviting smell of pumpkin pie. My mother and I were busy in the kitchen. Sweat dripped from my forehead as I waited beside the oven. It was almost 6, dinner should be served soon. The calendar beside the ticking wall clock showed the date, the 31st. It was that spooky time of the year, Halloween. The house was covered in black and orange decorations, and the scent of flavourful candy was all around our home. I still remember every single detail of that night. The night of my most tragic nightmare and the memory was as fresh as a daisy in my head.

My little brother, who was 8, went out trick-or-treating around the neighbourhood with my father. He looked so small in his over-sized jet black jumper, with a full skeleton pattern printed on it, knocking on every door while trying to act scary and menacing. He even runs, walks, and dances as if he was an actual skeleton, such a precious little being. Everytime he says ‘Boo!’ to his neighbours, the smile on our father’s face grew wider, he just brings that kind of joy around. The day was perfectly calm and lovely, in fact, it was wonderful.

However, the peace was soon washed away when a heavy dread settled at the bottom of my heart. The wall clock struck 7, they should be home by now. After all, our neighbourhood wasn’t that big, so an hour should be enough to knock on every single door, and say the usual phrase ‘Trick or treat?”. I realised I was biting my already-short nails, a negative habit I developed as a result of stress.

The rain started to fall, soft and light at first, but then grew louder till the raindrops were pounding on the concrete pavement outside. I was worried sick when I suddenly saw them running through the playground, across our house. Although the rain was heavy and it was starting to fog up the window, I could see that their faces were plastered with a smile, and I couldn’t help but smile back.

I dashed to the front door when I heard the sound of thunder. As I opened the door, I yelled ‘Come in!!’ to the both of them. They were making their way through the swing sets when my little brother stumbled onto the sand. As he tried to get up, it happened, lightning struck them, it was so bright, my eyes started to burn. My head was suddenly spinning and my vision was blurry. The loud sound had also deafen my ears, they were buzzing. Then, I realised that all I could do was scream, and so I did. I screamed till my voice was sore, and I felt my mother’s comforting hug, trying desperately to calm me. The rest of the incident was quite blurry, as my vision was temporarily gone.

The next morning, I opened my eyes and stared at a ceiling, different from the one in my room. I sat up too quickly and felt a jab of pain shooting down my spine. My back was aching, and so was my head. I looked around and realised that I was in a hospital bed. The thick curtains were draped infront of the tall, wide windows, blocking out most of the sunlight. This part of my memory was also quite blurry since I was only around the age of 12 then.

I could only recall the moment when my mother and I were told that my father and brother were gone. Their bodies were burnt, as black as the skies of starless nights, crisp, some parts into ashes. As I heard, my eyes were filled with tears, and I couldn’t help but let it all out. They were both gone from my world, my own family. I couldn’t bear it. My pathetic screams of rage and devastation echoed the hospital hallways, I was out of my mind. My mother helplessly cried, while the doctor desperately tried to calm us.

Till this day, the memory has stuck with me, while lightnings and thunders reminded me of the incident, thus making my astraphobia worse. I try, I really do try, to overcome my fear. I’ve been to countless therapy sessions with many different therapists, none of them help me for long. Most nights, I sleep under my covers, afraid that it might rain too heavily, causing lightning to struck. I do not want to cry anymore. I long to be helped.

32. Make a story about RA. Kartini​


Raden AdjengKartini (21 April 1879 –17 September 1904), sometimes known as Raden Ayu Kartini, was a prominent Indonesian national hero from Java. She was also a pioneer in the area of education for girls and women's rights for Indonesians.

Born into an aristocratic Javanese family in the Dutch East Indies, now Indonesia, she attended a Dutch-language primary school. She aspired to further education but the option was unavailable to her and other girls in Javanese society. She came into contact with various officials and influential people, including J.H. Abendanon, who was in charge of implementing the Dutch Ethical Policy.

Kartini wrote letters about her ideas and feelings, and they were published in a Dutch magazine and later as: Out of Darkness to Light, Women's Life in the Village, and Letters of a Javanese Princess. Her birthday is now celebrated as Kartini Day in Indonesia. She took an interest in mysticism and opposed polygamy. Her advocacy for the education of girls was continued by her sisters. Kartini Schools were named for her and a fund established in her name to fund the education of girls.

Kartini was born into an aristocratic Javanese family when Java was part of the Dutch colony of the Dutch East Indies. Kartini's father Sosroningrat became Regency Chief of Jepara. Kartini's father was originally the district chief of Mayong. Her mother Ngasirah was the daughter of Madirono and a teacher of religion in Telukawur. She was his first wife but not the most important one. At this time, polygamy was a common practice among the nobility. Colonial regulations required a regency chief to marry a member of the nobility. Since Ngasirah was not of sufficiently high nobility, Sosroningrat married a second time to Woerjan (Moerjam), a direct descendant of the Raja of Madura. After this second marriage, Kartini's father was elevated to Regency Chief of Jepara, replacing his second wife's own father, Tjitrowikromo.

Kartini was the fifth child and second-eldest daughter in a family of eleven, including half siblings. She was born into a family with a strong intellectual tradition. Her grandfather, Pangeran Ario Tjondronegoro IV, became a regency chief at the age of 25, while Kartini's older brother Sosrokartono was an accomplished linguist. Kartini's family allowed her to attend school until she was 12 years old. Here, among other subjects, she learned to speak Dutch, an unusual accomplishment for Javanese women at the time. After she turned 12 she was secluded (pingit) at home, a common practice among Javanese nobility, to prepare young girls for their marriage. During seclusion girls were not allowed to leave their parents' house until they were married, at which point authority over them was transferred to their husbands. Kartini's father was more lenient than some during his daughter's seclusion, giving her such privileges as embroidery lessons and occasional appearances in public for special events.

Letter by Kartini to Rosa Abendanon (fragment)

During her seclusion, Kartini continued to educate herself on her own. Because she could speak Dutch, she acquired several Dutch pen friends. One of them, a girl by the name of Rosa Abendanon, became a close friend. Books, newspapers, and European magazines fed Kartini's interest in European feminist thinking, and fostered the desire to improve the conditions of indigenous Indonesian women, who at that time had a very low social status.

Kartini's reading included the Semarang newspaper, to which she began to send contributions which were published. Before she was 20 she had read Max Havelaar and Love Letters by Multatuli. She also read De Stille Kracht (The Hidden Force) by Louis Couperus, the works of Frederik van Eeden, Augusta de Witt, the Romantic-Feminist author Goekoop de-Jong Van Eek, and an anti-war novel by Berta von Suttner, Die Waffen Nieder! (Lay Down Your Arms!). All were in Dutch.

“What are you doing?” the little girl exclaimed in wonderment.
