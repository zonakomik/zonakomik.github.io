31. Answer the questions 1.A cuboid water tank is 4 meters tall,4 meters long and 3 meters wide A.What is the volume of the tank in cubic centimeters? B.How many litres of water will the tank hold?


A. To find the volume of the cuboid water tank in cubic centimeters, you can use the formula for the volume of a cuboid, which is length × width × height. In this case, it's 4 meters × 3 meters × 4 meters. Make sure to convert meters to centimeters (1 meter = 100 centimeters) for consistency in units.

Volume = 4 m × 3 m × 4 m = 48 cubic meters

Now, convert cubic meters to cubic centimeters (1 cubic meter = 1,000,000 cubic centimeters):

48 cubic meters × 1,000,000 cubic centimeters/cubic meter = 48,000,000 cubic centimeters

B. To find how many liters of water the tank will hold, you can use the fact that 1 liter is equivalent to 1,000 cubic centimeters.

So, 48,000,000 cubic centimeters is equal to 48,000,000/1,000 = 48,000 liters. The tank will hold 48,000 liters of water.
